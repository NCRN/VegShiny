Forestry Importance Value

Forestry Importance values are an index which of the relative importance of tree species in a forested area. This index was develped by .... The index is made up of three components:

  • Relative Abundance

    Relative abundance (also known as relative density) measures the abundance of each species compared to the combined abundance of all species. For a given species "X", the relative abundance is:

    Relative abundance of X = (Abundance of X) / (Abundance of all Species)

    Abundance is typically measured as a the number of individual plants (trees, saplings etc.) found.

  • Relative Dominance
  • Relative Distribution
